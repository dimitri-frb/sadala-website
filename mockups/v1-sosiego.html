Sosiego

sosiego (n.), Spanish — a state of deep calm and clarity.

D
Dimitri · founder, Sadala
Writing from the plot. April 30, 2026.

I drove past this plot for three years before I bought it. 1,096 square metres of pine shade, eight minutes from Málaga centre, surrounded by the kind of silence you usually have to drive an hour to find.

I bought it before I had a plan. The plan came afterwards — slowly, with the trees, the slope, and the way the morning sun moves across the south corner deciding most of it for me.

This page is the project. Not a brochure. A working document I'll keep updating until the keys are handed over.

why this plot

Three things you only know if you walk it at sunrise.

Note 01
The eight stone pines on the south side are more than seventy years old. We're building around them, not through them. The kitchen window faces east because that's where their morning shadows already pointed. — D.
Note 02
You can hear cicadas before you see the road. Eight minutes from Málaga, but the city stops at the second bend. I tested it with a sound meter — 32 dB at noon. That's library quiet. In a city. — D.
Note 03
The plot has a natural fall of three metres from the road to the south corner. We're using that drop to embed the gym, sauna and cold plunge below grade — so that the living floor sits at the height of the pine canopy, not below it. — D.
The concept

Body. Mind. Soul.

Health is the new luxury. Sosiego is conceived as an instrument for living longer, clearer, calmer. Three pillars inform every square metre — the body you move through, the mind you sharpen, the soul you settle.

01 · Body
Move. Recover. Repeat.

Indoor + outdoor gym, lap pool, cold plunge, sauna, infrared cabin. Movement built into the architecture, not added later.

below grade. quieter. cooler.
02 · Mind
Think better. Rest deeper.

Library, meditation room, cinema, a Zen garden carved between the pines. Light studied for the actual orientation, never assumed.

my favourite room.
03 · Soul
Slow down. Tune in.

KM0 vegetable garden, olive grove, fire pit, summer kitchen. The Andalusian table, returned to the centre of the home.

where dinner happens.

The KM0 garden

The salad for tonight grows fifteen metres from the table.

Tomatoes, herbs, citrus, an olive grove that already throws shadow. The kind of small daily ritual you can't outsource — and the kind that makes you feel rooted somewhere within a week of moving in.

We're working with a local horticulturist from Casabermeja. She picked the varieties. They'll be in the ground before the keys are handed over.

— D.

If you've read this far,

you've probably been looking at houses long enough to recognise the feeling that none of them are quite right. The kitchen is in the wrong place. The garden is too small. The light is wrong. The people who built it didn't think about you.

Sosiego is the only home I'm building this year. It is being built by someone who would happily live in it himself. If that's not the right villa for you, I'll tell you so. If it is, we'll talk often — and you'll know the house long before the keys are in your hand.

Either way, the right way to start is a coffee on the plot. The pines say more in five minutes than this page does.
