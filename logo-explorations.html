Logo explorations

Six directions for the Sadala mark.

Wordmark stays. Symbol gets a fresh shape.

Sadala
01
The arch, refined.
A single line, drawn once. Echoes the Andalusian doorway, the threshold, the entrance to a home. Replaces the current "drawn building" feel with one clean gesture.
Sadala
02
Horizon & sun.
A line and a circle. The Mediterranean horizon at dawn, the sun rising over Pinares. Reads as a stamp - simple, repeatable, scales to a favicon without losing its identity.
Sadala
03
Monogram S.
Pure typography, no symbol. The italic serif S becomes the mark. Most editorial, most self-confident. Feels like a publishing house or a small wine domaine.
Sadala
04
Doorway, filled.
Solid arched doorway with a smaller arch carved out - a home within a home. More confident on small surfaces (favicon, business card). Slightly heavier than 01.
Sadala
05
The pines.
Three pines on a horizon - direct nod to Pinares de San Antón, the place behind every project. Most narrative of the six. Could pair beautifully with the "Rooted" principle.
Sadala
Málaga · Andalucía
06
No symbol at all.
A line, the wordmark, the place. Most minimal route. Trusts the typography to carry everything. Feels like a journal masthead - quiet, certain, unrepeatable.

In context: nav bar at the top of any page
